SURUKI SUPERMARKET
Address: 93 West 25th Ave, San Mateo, CA 94403
Type: Japanese Grocery / Prepared Foods
Hours: Generally Tuesday-Sunday, check current hours

Overview: Suruki Supermarket is a family-owned Japanese grocery store that has operated in San Mateo for several decades. It is smaller than chain Asian supermarkets like Ranch 99 or Mitsuwa, but its prepared foods section is considered by many regulars to be the best value in the city for quality sushi, sashimi, and Japanese deli items.

PREPARED FOODS COUNTER - WHAT TO ORDER
The deli counter at Suruki is the main event. Key items:
- Sushi and sashimi trays: Made fresh daily. The fish quality is consistently described as excellent and the pricing undercuts standalone sushi restaurants significantly.
- Bento boxes: Pre-packaged lunch bento with rice, protein (teriyaki salmon, chicken katsu, or similar), and pickled sides. These are popular with local office workers and sell out most days before 1 PM.
- Onigiri: Handmade rice balls with a variety of fillings. The salmon onigiri and the umeboshi (plum) onigiri are the most popular. Freshness is notably better than convenience store onigiri.
- Prepared side dishes: Pickled vegetables, seaweed salads, and house-made inari tofu pouches.

BEST DAYS AND TIMES TO VISIT
Suruki receives fish deliveries on Tuesday and Friday mornings. These are the best days to buy sashimi and sushi for peak freshness. Prepared bento boxes are available daily but sell out by early afternoon on most days. Arriving before noon is recommended for the best selection.

TAKAHASHI MARKET
Address: 221 S Claremont St, San Mateo, CA 94401
Type: Japanese-Hawaiian Grocery / Prepared Foods
Hours: Check current hours; typically closed Monday

Overview: Takahashi Market is one of the oldest surviving Japanese-Hawaiian markets in the Bay Area. The Takahashi family has operated the store for generations, and the market carries items that reflect both Japanese and Hawaiian culinary traditions -- a cultural combination that reflects San Mateo's historic Japanese-American community, many of whom have Hawaiian connections.

PREPARED FOODS - WHAT TO ORDER
- Plate lunches: Hawaiian-style plate lunches with two scoops of rice, macaroni salad, and a protein (chicken katsu, teriyaki beef, or kalua pork when available). A direct connection to the Hawaiian plate lunch tradition.
- Musubi: Spam musubi is a staple item. Simple, fresh, and a reliable snack.
- Bento boxes: Similar to Suruki but with more Hawaiian-influenced preparations.

THE FRIDAY MALASADA TRADITION
Takahashi's most famous offering is its fresh malasadas, available exclusively on Fridays. Malasadas are Portuguese-origin fried doughnuts that became a staple of Hawaiian cuisine through the Portuguese immigrant labor community in the Hawaiian sugar industry. They are soft, airy, fried to a golden brown, and rolled in granulated sugar while still warm.

Takahashi's malasadas are made fresh on Friday mornings only. They typically arrive at the counter between 9 AM and 10 AM and sell out by noon -- sometimes earlier if it is a busy Friday. There is no pre-order system for the Friday malasadas. Regulars know to arrive Friday morning to guarantee getting them. Showing up Friday afternoon is almost always too late.
