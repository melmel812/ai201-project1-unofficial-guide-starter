ABOUT SWEET MAPLE - SAN MATEO LOCATION

Sweet Maple is a Peninsula brunch institution with a devoted following built largely on a single iconic dish: Millionaire's Bacon. The San Mateo location at 2101 S El Camino Real is the primary Peninsula outpost of the brand, which originated in San Francisco.

MILLIONAIRE'S BACON
The signature item is thick-cut, caramelized bacon that is slow-baked with a blend of spices including brown sugar, black pepper, cayenne, and a proprietary spice mix. The result is a sweet-savory-spicy glazed bacon that caramelizes into a lacquered, almost candy-like consistency. It is served as a standalone item or as an add-on to egg dishes and plates. Nearly every table orders it. First-time visitors are universally advised to order Millionaire's Bacon regardless of what else they get.

WEEKEND OPERATIONS AND WAIT TIMES
Sweet Maple does not accept reservations for weekend brunch. This is a deliberate policy -- the restaurant operates on a first-come, first-served basis to maintain a specific kind of neighborhood energy and avoid the overhead of reservation management.

On Saturday and Sunday mornings, the wait time typically begins building before the restaurant opens at 8 AM. By 9 AM, waits of 30 to 45 minutes are standard. By 10 AM to noon (peak brunch window), waits of 45 minutes to over 1 hour are common. Locals recommend arriving at opening (8 AM) or waiting until 1:30 PM when the rush begins to thin. The restaurant stops seating new guests approximately 30-45 minutes before the 3 PM closing time.

The wait time experience: Sweet Maple provides a buzzer system and guests can wait outside or in the adjacent parking area. There is no adjacent indoor waiting area. On cold or rainy mornings this is a significant practical consideration.

MENU HIGHLIGHTS
- Millionaire's Bacon: The signature. Thick-cut, caramelized, spiced. Order it.
- French Toast: Thick-sliced brioche, seasonal preparations. One of the most commonly photographed items.
- Eggs Benedict: Multiple variations including a crab cake Benedict that is frequently cited in reviews.
- Huevos Rancheros: A California-Mexican fusion take on the classic, with house-made salsa.
- Pancakes: Buttermilk, with seasonal fruit preparations.
- Smoked Salmon Plate: Capers, cream cheese, red onion, bagel chips.

DIETARY NOTES
Sweet Maple is not a vegan-friendly restaurant. The menu is heavily oriented toward egg dishes, bacon, and dairy. Vegetarian options exist (egg dishes without meat, pancakes, French toast) but the kitchen is shared with meat preparations. Gluten-free options are limited -- confirm with staff for specific dishes.

PRICING
Brunch dishes range from approximately $14 to $24. Millionaire's Bacon as a standalone add-on is approximately $7-9. Coffee and juice are standard. The price point reflects the weekend brunch positioning -- this is a treat meal, not a daily affordable option.

LOCATION AND PARKING
The restaurant is on S El Camino Real, which means it is not within walking distance of the downtown Caltrain station. Driving or rideshare is the practical approach. There is a parking lot adjacent to the restaurant, but it fills up on weekend mornings and overflow parking requires circling the surrounding blocks.
